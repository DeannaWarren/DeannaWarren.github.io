Building a Website Reflection - Last Requirement - 11-8-15

DISCLAIMER: THE FOLLOWING IS FROM A SERIES OF POST MADE DURING THE FIRST HALF OF MY TIME AT DEV BOOT CAMP, AND THEREFORE MAY CONTAIN INCONSISTENCIES AND MISINFORMATION AS THEY ARE WRITTEN IN THE WEEK I LEARN A NEW CONCEPT. TAKE WITH A GRAIN OF SALT.

This is, I believe, my last required post with Dev Boot Camp. Today I'm going to reflect on the process of building a website.

I built this website to the best of my abilities. When I originally made it, using only HTML/CSS that I wrote, it looked like this:

At current moment, my website does not look like that. Nor is is where I'd like it to be. I transfered my website to a Foundation framework this week, and it was a lot more difficult that I expected. However, I was faced with the choice of working it out and having the tools to figure it out from here with a variety of premade CSS classes to work from, such as I used to create the navigational bar I'm using at the moment, or a simple class used to center text.

I would be interested in doing some more work on the site, like making my blog posts look a little more interesting, or fancying up the lists on my blog index and project index. And lord, do I have some writing to do to fix up my about me and the like! Luckily, my husband is taking a copywriting course, so I'm talking him into rewriting my stuff, because talking about yourself is so much harder than anything else.

I really enjoyed making this site, but I wish that I'd decided to go with the full framework rather than the CDN right away, because my limited experience with this framework ended up meaning I didn't know what which things to use, because I didn't know what I needed! I might be able to fix it up again with the CDN in the future, but for now, I just need to be able to take a breath, jump into the onsite portion of the course, and just be proud of what I've done.
